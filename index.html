Overview

This website presents evidence-based analysis of how Muslim communities in India, including Kashmiri Muslims, have benefited from government welfare programs, reservation inclusion, scholarships, healthcare, and financial inclusion initiatives.

Major Welfare Programs Benefiting Muslims

Financial Inclusion

Millions of Muslim households gained access to banking through Jan Dhan Yojana, enabling direct benefit transfers and financial independence.

Housing Support

Permanent housing provided through Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana improved living standards and asset ownership among Muslim beneficiaries.

Healthcare Access

Health insurance coverage up to ₹5 lakh per family through Ayushman Bharat improved healthcare access and reduced financial burden.

Education Scholarships

Scholarship programs increased education participation and career opportunities among Muslim students.

Reservation Inclusion for Kashmiri Muslims

Many Kashmiri Muslim communities were included in the Other Backward Classes (OBC) category, enabling access to reservation in education and government jobs.
Muslim tribal communities such as Gujjars and Bakerwals benefit from Scheduled Tribe reservation, providing education and employment opportunities.

Overall Impact

Major improvements include:
  • Increased access to housing
  • Healthcare coverage expansion
  • Educational scholarships
  • Financial inclusion
  • Reservation access expansion
  • Economic empowerment
